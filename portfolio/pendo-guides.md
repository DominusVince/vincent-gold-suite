# Pendo Guides: Designing In-App Learning Experiences

## Overview

As part of product user enablement strategy, I designed and developed a series of in-app guidance experiences using Pendo Guides.

The objective was to help users discover features, understand workflows, and complete tasks without having to leave the application or search through external documentation.

Rather than treating documentation and product experience as separate activities, the initiative focused on delivering learning directly within the user's workflow.

---

## The Challenge

Modern enterprise platforms often contain powerful capabilities that users may not immediately discover or fully understand.

Traditional documentation remains important, but users frequently prefer learning while they are actively performing a task.

Several challenges existed:

- Feature discoverability
- Workflow complexity
- New user onboarding
- Adoption of newly released capabilities
- Reducing dependency on external documentation

The goal was to provide contextual guidance that supported users at the point of need.

---

## My Role

I was responsible for planning, designing, and developing Pendo Guide content across multiple product areas.

My responsibilities included:

- Identifying user learning needs
- Understanding workflow pain points
- Collaborating with Product Managers and SMEs
- Designing guide structures and learning sequences
- Writing concise in-app instructional content
- Reviewing and refining user messaging
- Supporting feature adoption initiatives

---

## Approach

### 1. Understanding User Context

Before creating guides, I worked with Product Managers and stakeholders to understand:

- User goals
- Common tasks
- Frequently asked questions
- Areas of confusion
- Adoption challenges

This helped ensure the guidance addressed real user needs rather than assumptions.

---

### 2. Designing for Minimal Interruption

One of the key principles was respecting the user's workflow.

Guides were designed to be:

- Contextual
- Action-oriented
- Concise
- Easy to dismiss
- Focused on immediate value

The objective was to support users without overwhelming them.

---

### 3. Breaking Down Complex Workflows

Many platform workflows involved multiple configuration steps.

I simplified these experiences by:

- Identifying critical actions
- Sequencing instructions logically
- Removing unnecessary information
- Focusing on task completion

This reduced cognitive load and improved usability.

---

### 4. Supporting Product Adoption

Pendo Guides were also used to introduce new features and enhancements.

Rather than expecting users to read release notes, guidance was delivered directly within the product experience where it was most relevant.

This helped bridge the gap between feature availability and feature adoption.

---

## Outcome

The initiative contributed to a more self-service learning experience for users.

Benefits included:

- Improved feature discoverability
- Faster onboarding experiences
- Reduced friction during workflow execution
- Increased visibility of new capabilities
- Better alignment between product design and user education

The project demonstrated how in-app learning can complement traditional documentation and create a more seamless user experience.

## What I Learned

This project reinforced an important lesson:

Users rarely want documentation.

They want to complete tasks successfully.

The most effective learning experiences are often the ones that appear at the right moment, provide exactly the information needed, and disappear once the user has achieved their goal.

Designing Pendo Guides helped me think beyond traditional documentation and approach content as an integrated part of the overall product experience.
